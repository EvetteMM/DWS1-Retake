Chicken Cacciatore

by Evette Mestre Meyer

This rich and flavorful traditional Sicilian dish that is perfect for holidays or just a special night at home.

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut in eight pieces
  • 1 T salt
  • 1 t fresh ground black pepper
  • 1 t dried oregano
  • 1 T fresh minced garlic
  • 1/2 white distilled vinegar
  • 1/4 c extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 large yellow onion, slivered
  • 1 large green pepper, diced
  • 1 16 oz. can crushed tomatoes
  • 1/4 c sliced black olives, drained
  • 1/4 c petit capers, drained
  • 1/2 c good red wine, like Cabernet Sauvignon or Pinot Noir
  • 1/4 c fresh basil, chopped

Instructions

  1. Marinate chicken in salt, black pepper, oregano, minced garlic and vinegar for 1 hour.
  2. In a large skillet, brown the chicken on all sides in olive oil. (Discard marinade.) Keep
    moving in pan with tongs so the skin does not stick to the pan. Once browned, place in a
    large, deep ceramic baking dish.
  3. Saute the yellow onion and green pepper to the skillet. Scrape the pan to ensure you get
    all the brown bits in sauce. When the onions are clear, add the whole tomatoes.
  4. When the sauce begins to bubble, add the black olives, capers and red wine. Reduce
    heat and let simmer 10 minutes. Stir in fresh basil.
  5. Pour the sauce over the chicken. Cover the baking dish tightly with aluminum foil and
    bake for 45 minutes to 1 hour.

Accompaniments

A side of pasta or oven roasted potatoes are typically served with chicken cacciatore. For a lighter
healthier accompaniment, try baked spaghetti squash, wilted spinach or fresh steamed green beans.

Wine Pairings

  • Monteraponi Chianti Classico 2011
  • Castello di Ama Chianti Classico 2010
  • CADE Cabernet Sauvignon Howell Mountain 2009 (CA)
  • Col Solare 2008(WA)
